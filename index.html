Jolly Friendsmas!

A collection of albums curated for the people I love!

The Lemon of Pink

The Books

"Are we, like, major or minor anyway?"

  1. The Lemon of Pink I
  2. The Lemon of Pink II
  3. Tokyo
  4. Bonanza
  5. S Is For Evrysing
  6. Explanation Mark
  7. There Is No There
  8. Take Time
  9. Don't Even Sing About It
  10. The Future, Wouldn't That Be Nice
  11. A True Story of a Story of True Love
  12. That Right Ain't Shit
  13. PS

Many of these albums are a little bit weird, and a little bit obscure. That was intentional, as I wanted to have the highest chance of my friends finding new music in this little trove. The Lemon of Pink by The Books stands apart from these other albums. It sounds like nothing I had heard before and it introduced me to the niche genre of ‘collage-pop’, it is infectiously catchy, occasionally sad, and consistently funny. One of my favorite albums of all time, and I hope I’ll one day find another album that catches my mind in the same way.

How To Leave Town

Car Seat Headrest

"All my fantasies are faking orgasms, they're only in it for the money I made up for them"

  1. The Ending of Dramamine
  2. Beast Monster Thing (Love Isn't Love Enough)
  3. Kimochi Warui (When? When? When? When? When? When? When?)
  4. I-94 W (832 mi)
  5. You're In Love With Me
  6. America (Never Been)
  7. I Want You To Know That I'm Awake/I Hope That You're Asleep
  8. is this dust really from the Titanic?
  9. Hey, Space Cadet (Beast Monster Thing In Space)

I went on a pretty deep dive into Car Seat Headrest this year, and I think beyond his latest album this is his most accessible. Be warned that the first track has a rather lengthy synth/drum intro that last around 7 minutes. But after that, it’s smooth sailing through an album that really encapsulates the feelings of uncertainty relating to personal income, friends, relationships, addiction, and mental health. And while this sounds rather depressing, it’s presented in a disaffected and humorous tone that makes the album incredibly listenable and addictive.

I was introduced to this group by a friend of mine reciting the entire intro verse of Rainbow in the Dark to me in a spurt of hope. The hope was that I would know the second part of the dialogue that occurs during that verse. I unfortunately had never heard of the Indian Rap group Das Racist, let alone know the lyrics to one of their songs. So, with this experience behind me I decided that I should endeavor to learn this set of lyrics, so I don’t let down my friend. I’ve yet to memorize them, but in the meantime, this album is hella tight, and pretty funny to boot.

holo pleasures

elvis depressedly

"if there's a cool spot in hell, i hope you get it"

  1. okay
  2. pepsi/coke suicide
  3. inside you
  4. teeth
  5. weird honey
  6. thinning out

This is sunday morning hangover music, mixed with late night rainy driving music. A distant warm sound with a distinctive vocalist and some pretty instrumentation accompanied by some pretty basic song structure. One of my favorite aspects of this album is its length, it doesn’t “overstay its welcome” as many critics like to say. And while I don’t love that little saying, it does fit this album quite well. This album also has a sister album that is worth a listen if you’re hungry for more smooth fuzzy jams.

Luciferian Towers

Godspeed You! Black Emperor

"[Frightening sounds of corporations and militarism]"

  1. Undoing A Luciferian Towers
  2. Bosses Hang, Pt. I
  3. Bosses Hang, Pt. II
  4. Bosses Hang, Pt. III
  5. Fam/Famine
  6. Anthem For No State, Pt. I
  7. Anthem For No State, Pt. II
  8. Anthem For No State, Pt. III

Aw man, what is there to say about Godspeed You! Black Emperor. A canadian band that spans almost three decades and is one of the pioneers of post-rock. This is another band that I really dove head first into these past twelve months, and I would recommend everyone at least try out their album F#A# (Infinity). However, what I present to you here is their latest album, from this year, Luciferian Towers. A bleak but ultimately rebellious album which rallies against a world increasingly inundated with violence. The liner notes for this album are definitely worth a read before diving in.

History of Man

Illangelo

"[Monster Noise! Electronic cacaphony follows]"

  1. One Dreamy Hum
  2. Crash Landing
  3. The Escape
  4. Farewell
  5. The Haunting
  6. War
  7. Seven Phases
  8. A Strange World
  9. Shattered Paradise
  10. The Forsaken Ones

An electronic horror dance album that I only stumbled upon about a month before writing this. History of Man uses a bunch of well known tropes from the glitch sub-genre of electronic and melds them with an utterly unsettling tone, breakneck tempo, and the occasional psychedelic tinge which results in a nearly indescribable album that I am fascinated by. Best listened to in one sitting, preferably on a cold night.

On The Echoing Green

Jefre Cantu-Ledesma

"[Warm Ghostly Fuzz]"

  1. In A Copse
  2. A Song Of Summer
  3. Echoing Green
  4. The Faun
  5. Tenderness
  6. Vulgar Latin
  7. Autumn
  8. Dancers At The Spring
  9. Door To Night

Let the fuzz consume you! On The Echoing Green is an incredibly pleasant shoegaze daydream that washes over you with undulating reverb and warm distortion. An album whose album art is a wonderful representation of the mood of the album: abstract, colourful, simple. This music has the uncanny ability to transport me to the feeling of laying in the grass under a tree during the late afternoon of a summer day. Even now as I write this and listen to Tenderness, I have become noticeably more relaxed, and I’m fighting the urge to not close my eyes and drift into the fuzz once more.

This man is a goddamn national treasure and is the best lyricist this side of the 49th parallel.

Perils from the Sea

Mark Kozelek & Jimmy LaValle

"Kids are fucking pricks"

  1. What Happened to My Brother
  2. 1936
  3. Gustavo
  4. Baby in Death Can I Rest Next to Your Grave
  5. Ceiling Gazing
  6. You Missed My Heart
  7. Caroline
  8. He Always Felt Like Dancing
  9. By the Time That I Awoke
  10. Here Come More Perils from the Sea
  11. Somehow the Wonder of Life Prevails

Sun Kil Moon and The Album Leaf collide in an album full of narrative vignettes that meander about and stare at the ceiling, wondering why they’re telling themselves to you at all. I originally listened to this album in the background while I worked, but on my second or third listen I started to latch onto a few distinct lyrics that stood out amongst the rambling. And by god, did I get sucked in. I couldn’t focus on my work anymore once I got wrapped up in the little stories and their vivid descriptions. Put some headphones on, shut off the lights, and drift into another world.

BOOM! NEW GENRE THAT I HADN’T HEARD OF! DOWN TEMPO! Damn is this stuff ear candy. Warm bass, clear vocals, huge synths, and crystalline production. Sufjan Stevens and a bunch of other people got together and said to each other “Hey, y’know that thing called THE MILKY WAY GALAXY? Also Mercury is one of the top 5 singles of the year.

God, Forgive These Bastards

The Taxpayers

"And of course, I know you could have done much better, and I know that I must have been your bad habit. Some rotten man. Nobody's savior. Your oldest friend."

  1. As the Sun Beat Down
  2. Atlanta's Own

  12. I Love You Like An Alcoholic

A terribly sad punk album that follows the tragic life of a man named Henry Turner. The album is accompanied by a short story that sets the stage for the songs that follow, and it really does the album a disservice if you don’t read it before giving it a listen. Beyond this, the album blends some great elements of jazz, punk, folk, and even has some pre-recorded interviews to paint a compelling and detailed picture of a man’s entire life.

Always Foreign

The World is a Beautiful Place & I am No Longer Afraid to Die

"Can you still call it a country, if all the states are broken? Can you still call it a business, if all you do is steal?"

  1. I'll Make Everything
  2. The Future
  3. Hilltopper
  4. Faker
  5. Gram
  6. Dillon and Her Son
  7. Blank #12
  8. For Robin
  9. Marine Tigers
  10. Fuzz Minor
  11. Infinite Steve

You may be sitting back and thinking “Man, what is happening with emo rock these days?”. Well, Modern Baseball broke up, and The Smith Street Band is all the way in Australia so the only kinda well known band left is one with an overly long name. Luckily, their new album is pretty dang solid. TWIABPAIANLATD digs their heels into social commentary while keeping their penchant for large sweeping instrumentation, dramatic vocal arrangements, and a few long songs that go through two or three major movements. Overall Always Foreign gives me hope that one of the most prominent genres of our childhood will live on and evolve into something greater THAN IT EVER WAS BEFORE!!!!
